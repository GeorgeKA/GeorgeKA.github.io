Condominium Board Director

As a certified Director on a Toronto condominium board, I meet regularly with my team to provide a quality living experience for the residents of over 500 units.
Directors have a fiduciary responsibility to act in the best interest of unit owners. To that end, directors oversee and implement a variety of tasks.

At its most basic, the board must maintain the physical property, which includes soliciting quotes from vendors, assessing proposals, and collaborating with our regular contractors.
Everyone involved from residents to condominium managers must comply with the Condominium Act, by-laws, and rules. Directors must make a reasonable effort to ensure this.
Resident safety can't be overlooked. We work closely with the local police department to improve security.
Unit owners must be kept up to date with annual meetings, and ad hoc communication.
And of course there's no such thing as a free lunch. It's a prime responsibility to conduct all of the board's tasks while managing the operating budget and reserve fund.

Gardening

Environmental sustainability and self reliance have been interests of mine for some time now. To that end, I endeavour to optimize the small space available to me to grow those passions.
In keeping with sustainability, I craft my own soil blend with compost from the city, sustainably sourced coconut coir (instead of unsustainably harvested perlite), and vermiculite. Also, I employ a version of hugelkultur in several of my raised beds to reduce soil requirements, and increase water retention. Mulching with straw also goes a long way when it comes to water retention, while doubling as an organic matter amendment.

I employ many other techniques like companion planting and drip irrigation to produce practical and bountiful harvests.
