OmNomNoms

In my daily pursuit of health, I had a need for an easy way to compare the macronutrient details of foods. The common nutrition applications available weren't up to the task, so I took the initiative to make it for myself.


OmNomNoms is a simple application that allows you to compare different foods' macronutrient values and empowers you to easily swap new foods into your diet while sticking to your diet plan. Aside from its other secondary features, you maintain complete control of your data, so no more second guessing the validity of the data that you depend on. You start off with a robust foundation of data, upon which you can add to your heart's desire.

GIS - Infection Tracking

My final year project had my team working on a GIS (geographic information system) for infection tracking within hospitals, in partnership RL Solutions. The final product was a success and brought us 2nd place in the faculty's competition.
The goal of this project is to provide hospitals with a tool to track the spread of hospital acquired infections within hospitals. We accomplished this with the use of RFID (radio frequency ID) readers and tags.

Essentially, all occupants within the given hospital, (and specialty equipment), would be given small clothes-pin RFID tag wearables to wear during their stay within the building. Using the interactions of individuals, combined with our research of common hospital infections (C. Diff and MRSA), we provide the user with real time infection probabilities of individuals. And to boot, the system easily doubles as a hospital information system!
