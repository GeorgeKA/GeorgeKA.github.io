IBM

From 2020 until mid 2024 I was a software developer on the Db2 development team. The specific team that I worked on was the Operating System Services team. This team handled the layer between Db2 and the operating system by providing operating system function wrappers for upper level Db2 components, handling memory management tools, and locking mechanisms.

The role largely involved defect analysis, customer issue investigations, and new development projects. As team lead and scrum master I improved team cohesion and productivity by running weekly scrums, presenting skills sharing sessions to junior colleagues, and organizing our work items within GitHub & Zenhub.

Most recently I worked on improvements to Db2's INGEST command, the details of which should be public in the coming months. The INGEST tool is a utility that simplifies the transport of data sets into tables without impact to running Db2 applications. While working on this and other projects in the past, I worked within an agile framework. We would start by identifying project requirements, providing sizing, (either with concrete dates, or via story points with PlanningPoker), and developing iteratively within sprints.

Prior to working in development, in 2015 I joined IBM, (also within their Db2 team), but as a Technical Support Engineer. This role provided me the opportunity to engage head on with our customers. I had to leverage my technical knowledge of the product and software development, while making use of robust communication skills.

Working with customers' mission critical systems sometimes makes for high pressure environments where the stakes are high. As a result, I have experience making rational decisions under time constraints to maximize success. I know how to set expectations with stakeholders, and identify priorities.

OmNomNoms

The available nutrition apps weren't up to the task, so I took the initiative to develop one myself.


OmNomNoms is a simple application that allows you to compare different foods' macronutrient values and it empowers you to easily swap new foods into your diet while sticking to your diet plan. Aside from its secondary features, you maintain complete control of your data, so no more second guessing the validity of the data that you depend on. You start off with a robust foundation of data, upon which you can add to your heart's desire.

GIS - Infection Tracking

My final year project had my team working on a GIS (geographic information system) for infection tracking within hospitals, in partnership with RL Solutions. The final product was a success and brought us 2nd place in the faculty's competition.

The goal of this project was to provide hospitals with a tool to track the spread of hospital-acquired infections. We accomplished this with the use of RFID (radio frequency ID) readers and tags.

Essentially, all occupants and specialty equipment within a given hospital would be given small clothes-pin RFID tag wearables to wear during their stay within the building. Using the interactions of individuals, combined with our research of common hospital infections, (C. Diff and MRSA), we provided real time infection probabilities of occupants. And to boot, the system easily doubles as a hospital information system.
